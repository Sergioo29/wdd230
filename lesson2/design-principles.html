Hick's Law

Spotify

spotify.com

The standard dark mode is really appealing. I personally always look for a dark mode on my apps. The header is simple, it has less than 4 options, with a big button in high contrast. The page content is straight to the point: album cover images for you to find your songs. Simple design, few options, easy user decision-making.

Fitt's Law

Trinket

trinket.io

I believe that is called hero image. The hero image has a bright color that calls our attention. Then, we have the "call to action" button in light green that is centralized. The button is big, but just right. Their developers used contrast to get the user to press that button, but no exaggeration.

White Space and Clean Design

Google

Google.com

Google, what else can I say? The Google website is one of, maybe the most perfect example of a good website. The options under the search bar may vary depending on what device the user is using. There is also a header with user log in stardards, but it is quite hidden on the top right corner, so it does not take away the focus. The high amount of white space have all hour attention to the search bar.
